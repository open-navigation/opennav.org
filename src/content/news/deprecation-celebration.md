This article celebrates the end-of-life status of ROS 2 Foxy Fitzroy (June 2023) and reflects on its significance for robotics development.

![ROS 2 Foxy End of Life](/images/news/deprecation.png)

## The Milestone

Foxy was the first ROS 2 distribution that we considered truly production-ready. Nav2 achieved its v1.0 milestone in early 2021, enabling widespread adoption across logistics, agriculture, mining, and construction sectors.

## The Challenge

While Foxy was groundbreaking, maintaining it became increasingly difficult. The framework evolved significantly, making it nearly impossible to backport newer features while preserving backward compatibility. We repeatedly told users: "Please upgrade to Humble or newer for [insert feature]."

## Progress Since Foxy

Modern Nav2 distributions now include:

- **Action feedback and contextual error codes** for better debugging
- **Costmap Filters** for zone management
- **Advanced path planning** for various robot types
- **Python3 API** for system interaction
- **30% more packages** and **91% unit test coverage**

## What This Means for You

If you're still on Foxy, now is the time to upgrade. The latest LTS distribution (Humble) offers significantly more features, better performance, and continued support.

## Thank You

We thank Open Robotics for their work on ROS 2, and our partner companies like Dexory and Polymath Robotics for their continued support.

Here's to moving forward!
